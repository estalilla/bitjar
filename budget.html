'Failing to plan is planning to fail' - Proverb

The first step in taking control of your finances is to assess just what exactly your finances are. In other, create a budget. We'll go into detail and construct a budget for you shortly, but first let's go over some basics.

Necessary vs. Discretionary (or Need vs. Want)

Certain expenses are unavoidable and can even be good for you. For example, you need food. Food costs money (unless you are a farmer or some sort of hunter/gatherer) and buying good food can have positive effects on your health. Other basic needs typically require money as well. The largest tend to be food and shelter (i.e. rent). A few other big ones include clothing, transportation, and trips to the doctor.

Then there is the stuff you want, but can still exist without. These are known as discretionary expenses because we can decide to buy or forego them at any point. Examples such as designer clothes, new cars, or even your iPhone are luxuries (although some may argue that their smartphone is practically a vital appendage at this point). These are not bad things to purchases, but it's important that you first make sure you can afford to cover your basic needs.

'Something poignant' - ME

If you work in the United States you will be paying a portion of your monthly paycheck to the government in the form of several types of taxes. Federal taxes are generally the largest depending your total income, followed by social security and state taxes (for states that have income taxes). Finally, Medicare and other health insurance payments may be taken out of your paycheck before you even see any of the money.
